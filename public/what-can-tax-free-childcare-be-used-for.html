What Can You Use Tax-Free Childcare For?

Tax-Free Childcare can be used for a wide range of registered childcare costs — not just nurseries. Here's the complete list of what qualifies and what doesn't.

Last updated: March 2025 · Based on 2024/25 HMRC rules

What Tax-Free Childcare covers

  • ✓
    Nurseries and day nurseries — Ofsted-registered nurseries are the most common use
  • ✓
    Registered childminders — childminders registered with Ofsted or a childminder agency
  • ✓
    Pre-schools and playgroups — Ofsted-registered settings
  • ✓
    After-school clubs — Ofsted-registered after-school care
  • ✓
    Breakfast clubs — Ofsted-registered providers
  • ✓
    Holiday clubs and camps — Ofsted-registered holiday childcare providers
  • ✓
    Registered nannies — nannies registered through an Ofsted-registered nanny agency

What Tax-Free Childcare does NOT cover

  • ✕
    School fees or school meals — state or private school tuition is not eligible
  • ✕
    Unregistered babysitters or au pairs — must be Ofsted registered
  • ✕
    School trips or uniforms — not childcare costs
  • ✕
    Tutoring or extracurricular activities — unless delivered by a registered childcare provider
  • ✕
    Parent and toddler groups — unless the setting is Ofsted registered as a childcare provider

The key test: The provider must be registered with Ofsted (or the equivalent regulator in Scotland, Wales, or Northern Ireland) as a childcare provider. If in doubt, ask your provider for their Ofsted registration number before making a payment.

Holiday clubs — a hidden opportunity

Many parents don't realise that holiday clubs and activity camps qualify for Tax-Free Childcare, provided they are Ofsted registered. This makes the summer holidays significantly cheaper. Always check the provider is registered before booking — many are, and will advertise this.

How to pay a provider

Log into your Tax-Free Childcare account, enter your provider's Ofsted registration number, and make the payment directly. The payment leaves your account (including government top-up) and arrives with the provider within 3–5 working days.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use Tax-Free Childcare for after-school clubs?

Yes — Ofsted-registered after-school clubs and breakfast clubs are eligible for Tax-Free Childcare payments.

Does Tax-Free Childcare cover holiday camps?

Yes — Ofsted-registered holiday clubs and activity camps qualify. Always check the provider's registration before booking.

Can I use Tax-Free Childcare for private school fees?

No — school fees are not eligible. Tax-Free Childcare can only be used for registered childcare costs, not education fees.
