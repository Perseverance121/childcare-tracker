30-Hour Free Childcare for Single Parents: What You're Entitled To

Single parents are fully eligible for 30-hour free childcare and Tax-Free Childcare — and only need to meet the income criteria themselves, not as a couple. Here's what you're entitled to.

Last updated: March 2025 · Based on 2024/25 HMRC rules

Single parent advantage: Only you need to meet the minimum earnings threshold — not a second parent. And only your income is assessed against the £100,000 upper limit.

Single parent eligibility rules

In a single-parent household, the eligibility rules are simpler: only you need to be working and meeting the income requirements. There's no second parent to worry about.

  • ✓
    You are working — employed, self-employed, or on leave
  • ✓
    You earn at least £9,518/year — or are on maternity/parental leave
  • ✓
    Your adjusted net income is under £100,000
  • ✓
    Your child is the right age — 3 or 4 years old (or 9 months+ under the expanded scheme)

What counts as a single-parent household?

For HMRC's purposes, you're a single parent if you are the sole claimant and there is no partner living with you. If you share care of the child with an ex-partner, you can still claim as a single parent — but only one parent can hold the childcare code for a given child at any time.

Shared care arrangements

If your child splits time between two households, each parent can have their own Tax-Free Childcare account — but only one 30-hour code can be used per child. Typically the primary carer holds the code. It's worth discussing with your childcare provider who the code is registered to.

Universal Credit and free childcare

If you receive Universal Credit, you may be entitled to support with childcare costs through UC rather than (or in addition to) Tax-Free Childcare. UC can cover up to 85% of eligible childcare costs. You generally cannot receive both UC childcare support and Tax-Free Childcare top-ups for the same costs — choose the more beneficial option.

Universal Credit claimants: Check carefully which scheme gives you more benefit before applying for Tax-Free Childcare. If UC covers 85% of your costs, it may be worth more than the 20% TFC top-up.

Frequently asked questions

Can single parents get 30 hours free childcare?

Yes — single parents are fully eligible. Only you need to meet the income criteria. There's no requirement for a second parent to be working.

What if I work part-time?

As long as your part-time earnings meet the minimum threshold of £9,518 per year (equivalent to 16 hours at National Living Wage), you qualify.

Can I get free childcare if my ex-partner earns over £100,000?

Your ex-partner's income is not assessed for your eligibility. Only your adjusted net income counts — as long as you're under £100,000, you qualify regardless of your ex-partner's earnings.
