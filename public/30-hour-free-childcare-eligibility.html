30-Hour Free Childcare Eligibility: Do You Qualify?

The 30-hour free childcare scheme gives eligible working parents in England up to 1,140 free hours of childcare per year. Here's exactly who qualifies, what the income rules are, and how to check your eligibility.

Who qualifies for 30 hours free childcare?

To be eligible for the 30-hour entitlement in England, you need to meet all of the following criteria:

  • ✓
    Both parents are working — in a two-parent household, both must be in work. Single parents only need to meet the criteria themselves.
  • ✓
    Each parent earns at least £9,518/year — equivalent to 16 hours per week at the National Living Wage (£11.44/hr for 2024/25).
  • ✓
    Neither parent earns over £100,000 — this is based on adjusted net income, not gross salary.
  • ✓
    Your child is 3 or 4 years old — or an eligible 2-year-old under the expanded scheme.
  • ✓
    You live in England — Scotland, Wales, and Northern Ireland have separate schemes with different rules.

The minimum earnings rule explained

Each working parent must earn the equivalent of 16 hours per week at the National Living Wage. For 2024/25 that works out to approximately £9,518 per year or £793/month.

This is averaged over a three-month period, so your earnings don't need to be identical each month. If you work irregular hours or are self-employed, HMRC looks at your expected earnings over the coming quarter.

On maternity or paternity leave? You automatically meet the minimum earnings threshold. Parents on maternity leave, paternity leave, or shared parental leave are treated as if they are working for eligibility purposes.

The £100,000 income limit

If either parent has an adjusted net income above £100,000, the household loses eligibility entirely. Adjusted net income is your total taxable income minus pension contributions made through salary sacrifice, gift aid donations, and trading losses.

Earning close to £100,000? Increasing your pension contributions through salary sacrifice can reduce your adjusted net income below the threshold — restoring eligibility. Every £1 of additional pension sacrifice saves you up to 60p in tax and NI, and unlocks the 30-hour entitlement worth thousands per year.

Which children are eligible?

The 30-hour entitlement is available for 3 and 4 year olds in England. From April 2024, the government expanded free childcare to younger children in phases:

  • ✓
    From April 2024 — eligible 2-year-olds of working parents
  • ✓
    From September 2024 — children aged 9 months to 2 years of working parents
  • ✓
    3 and 4 year olds — 30 hours per week for working parents

Children become eligible from the term following their relevant birthday. The three term start dates in England are 1 January, 1 April, and 1 September.

Frequently asked questions

Can I use 30 hours free childcare and Tax-Free Childcare together?

Yes — they work alongside each other. Use your 30-hour code for the free sessions and your Tax-Free Childcare account to pay for additional hours, meals, or holiday care.

What if I'm self-employed?

Self-employed parents are eligible as long as your expected profit meets the minimum earnings threshold. HMRC assesses your expected earnings for the coming quarter.

How do I apply?

Apply online via gov.uk/tax-free-childcare using your Government Gateway account. HMRC will issue you a 30-hour code to give to your childcare provider. You must reconfirm eligibility every 3 months.

Does the 30-hour entitlement cover school holidays?

The standard 30-hour entitlement covers 38 term weeks per year. Some providers offer stretched hours across the full year (typically around 22 hours per week over 52 weeks), but this varies by provider.
