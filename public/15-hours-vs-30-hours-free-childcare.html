15 Hours vs 30 Hours Free Childcare: What's the Difference?

All 3 and 4 year olds in England are entitled to 15 hours of free childcare. But working parents can double this to 30 hours. Here's how both entitlements work and how to claim them.

The universal 15-hour entitlement

Every child aged 3 or 4 in England is entitled to 15 hours of free early education per week for 38 weeks per year — regardless of whether their parents work. This is called the universal entitlement and there are no income requirements. You simply register with an Ofsted-registered provider.

The working parents 30-hour entitlement

Working parents who meet the income criteria can access an additional 15 hours — 30 hours in total. The extra 15 hours is called the extended entitlement. To access it, both parents must be working and meet the minimum and maximum income thresholds.

Think of it this way: The first 15 hours is a universal right for your child. The second 15 hours is an employment benefit for working parents. You need to apply for the second 15 hours via HMRC — the first 15 hours you just register directly with a provider.

How to get the 15-hour universal entitlement

Contact any Ofsted-registered nursery, childminder, or pre-school and tell them your child is 3 or 4. They will arrange the funded hours directly with your local authority. You don't need to apply through HMRC.

How to get the 30-hour extended entitlement

Apply through the HMRC Childcare Service at gov.uk/tax-free-childcare. You'll receive a 30-hour code to give to your provider. You must reconfirm eligibility every 3 months.

The new expanded entitlement from September 2024

From September 2024, working parents of children aged 9 months to 2 years are entitled to 15 free hours per week. This will increase to 30 hours per week for eligible children aged 9 months to school age from September 2025.

Frequently asked questions

Do all children get 15 hours free childcare?

Yes — all 3 and 4 year olds in England get 15 hours per week of free early education for 38 weeks per year, regardless of parental income or employment.

Can I get 30 hours if only one parent works?

In a two-parent household, both parents must be working. If only one parent works, you only qualify for the universal 15-hour entitlement.

Is the 30-hour entitlement really free?

The funded hours are free, but some providers charge for meals, consumables, or additional services. Always check with your provider what is included.
